Cleaning Houses

My mom hired me on for the summer when I was 14-years old to help clean her clients houses. I didn't make much, and I honestly hated doing it, but it was a job. It was the first time I actually was making my own money and would brag to my friends when I was younger about how cool I was already having a job.

Pike's Gas Station

The first "real" job I actually had to go in and get interviewed for and work for someone else was a gas station a few blocks away from my house. The pay was actually worse than what my mom was giving me, but I could walk to work and after school as well. I mainly worked weekends because of basketball, but there were mostly kids around my age working there so it was fun being able to hangout with them while working.

Westside Tire

After highschool I didn't know what I wanted to do, and I wasn't planning to go to college without a plan. So right out of school, I landed a job as a delivery driver at a place over in Plymouth. It paid well for how old I was and I got to work alone all day. I would driver all of there state for around 4-6 hours everyday.

Ultra Machining Company (UMC)

This was the last job I had before I came back to school. I can't say too much because most of the things we did there and made are for confidential clients. But I can say things like, "one of our clients puts rockets in the sky and goes to space" if that answers some questions. It was really cool though. I got to work around the shop on machines and see blueprints for certain parts we made for our clients. It actually made me think about being a machinist for awhile, and they would've even paid for my schooling. But I knew in the long-term that computer science is what I wanted to do.
